### **Analysis of Pricing Conventions**

Research into successful Software as a Service (SaaS) applications, particularly in the project management and inventory sectors (like Trello and Zoho Inventory), reveals several key conventions:

* **Value-Based Tiers:** Pricing tiers are most effective when they align with distinct customer segments. Tiers are typically differentiated by a "value metric"—a unit of consumption that scales with the customer's needs. Common metrics include the number of users, number of items (projects, boxes), or access to advanced, resource-intensive features.
* **Feature Gating:** Core functionalities are often offered in lower-priced or free tiers to attract a wide user base, while advanced, complex, or high-cost features are reserved for premium tiers. This creates a clear and compelling path for users to upgrade as their needs become more sophisticated.
* **The "Freemium" vs. "Free Trial" Choice:**
    * **Freemium (Permanent Free Tier):** Works well for products with network effects or a very simple core value that can be demonstrated with limited features.
    * **Free Trial:** More effective for complex applications where users need to experience the full suite of features to understand the product's value. The time limit creates urgency and typically leads to higher conversion rates from free to paid users.
* **The Power of Three (or Four):** Most companies present 3-4 paid tiers, often including a free or trial option. This structure avoids overwhelming users with too many choices while still providing options for different needs and budgets.

Applying these conventions to "Smooth Moves," a one-time fee per-move model is most appropriate, as the service is tied to a specific, time-bound event rather than ongoing use. A free trial is the best entry point, as the app's full value is realized when a user engages with its interconnected features like the planner, budgeter, and box management systems.

### **Proposed Pricing Tiers for Smooth Moves**

Here is a 3-tier model with a free trial, structured to provide increasing value and cater to different types of movers.

#### **1. Free Trial**

* **Target Audience:** All potential users who want to evaluate the application's core value before committing.
* **Use Case:** A user is planning a move and wants to try out the main organizational tools to see if the app is a good fit. They can set up their move, add a few boxes, and create some tasks to understand the workflow.
* **Offer:**
    * **Duration:** 14-day full-featured access to the **"Essential Move"** tier.
    * **Post-Trial:** After the trial period, all data is preserved but becomes read-only. The user must upgrade to a paid plan to continue adding, editing, or managing their move. This "data-lock" is a strong incentive to convert.
