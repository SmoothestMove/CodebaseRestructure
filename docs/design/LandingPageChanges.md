# **Landing Page Changes Doc**

*Disclaimer: List is organized in the order in which each section should be organized, from top to bottom.*

---

### 1. Hero Section

* Keep revolving text (e.g., Budgeting, Planning, Relocating) but ensure a static subline clarifies the product at a glance.
* Make primary CTA ("Join the waitlist") more prominent with a subtle glowing/animated orange effect.
* Remove or reduce secondary CTAs (e.g., "Spread the word") to avoid distraction.
* Suggested copy for subline: *“Plan, budget, and organize every step of your move in one simple workspace.”*

---

### 2. Before vs. After Comparison

* Retain "Before Smooth Moves / After Smooth Moves" structure.
* Ensure concise bullet points are scannable.
* Consider light visual icons beside each list for faster comprehension.
* Suggested headline copy: *“From juggling chaos to smooth control.”*

---

### 3. Workflow / Product Showcase

* Use current screenshots but add short, punchy captions that explain benefits, not just features.
* Highlight differentiators (why Smooth Moves beats spreadsheets or Notion/Trello setups).
* Add subtle hover/zoom animations on screenshots for modern interactivity.
* Suggested caption example: *“Keep your budget, checklist, and packing plan all in one place.”*

---

### 4. Persona Proof Section ("Your Move, Your Story")

* Switch to a **bento grid layout** (3x2) for variety and visual rhythm.
* Add hover effects: slight pop, shadow, or playful animation.
* Use simple icons/illustrations for each persona (family, student, couple, etc.).
* Maintain inclusive personas (Family, Young Professional, Student, Couple, Remote Worker, Downsizing).
* End section with a unifying CTA: *“Join the waitlist and help shape Smooth Moves for your journey.”*

---

### 5. Waitlist Tiers (Replaces Pricing Section)

* Convert pricing layout into a **3-tier waitlist structure**.
* Each tier reflects different levels of involvement and feedback depth:

  * **Quick Check** – Lightweight testing. Report bugs only. ~5 minutes/week.
  * **Feedback Friend** – Bug testing + open-ended response: *“Describe your experience. What worked? What could be better?”* ~15 minutes/week.
  * **Deep Dive** – Bug testing + open-ended response + structured questionnaire about features and usability. ~30+ minutes/week.
* Keep the 3-column layout from pricing to visually communicate choice.
* Ensure messaging emphasizes that **all levels matter**—users are free to choose their comfort zone.
* Allow for movement between tiers later (e.g., a Quick Check tester can grow into a Deep Dive tester).
* Suggested header copy: *“Choose your level of involvement—every voice matters.”*

---

### 6. About / Founder’s Note

* Since the design is a one-page layout, keep this section integrated rather than linking out to a separate page.
* Condense to a **short but powerful story snippet** (2–3 sentences) with a friendly, authentic tone.
* Use a small photo/avatar of the founder to add personality and trust.
* Position it as a standalone section with a clear header like *“My Story”* to distinguish it from functional/product sections.
* Suggested short copy: *“I built Smooth Moves after my own chaotic relocation. What started as sticky notes and spreadsheets turned into the app I wish I had. Now I’m sharing it with you.”*

---

### 7. FAQ Section

* Rewrite in conversational tone (empathetic, clear, no corporate jargon).
* Add FAQ addressing skepticism directly (e.g., “Is this just another app I’ll forget about?”).
* Emphasize simplicity, privacy, and flexibility in answers.
* Suggested FAQ opener: *“Let’s clear up the questions you’re probably already asking.”*

---

### 8. Closing Section (CTA)

* Strengthen copy for urgency and empathy: *“Moving day doesn’t have to be chaos. Smooth Moves keeps you on track from start to finish.”*
* Add small supportive visual (moving boxes, suitcase, checklist).
* CTA button repeats glowing orange effect from hero for consistency.
* Suggested closing CTA copy: *“Join early access today and make your next move your smoothest move yet.”*
