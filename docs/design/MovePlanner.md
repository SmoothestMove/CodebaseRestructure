App Setup & Navigation

•	Get Comfortable with the Apps Navigation: Explore the apps features, check and set settings, and get ready to customize your move as you see fit.

•	Define Spaces & Create Owners: Organize your move by adding rooms to the Spaces page as needed and create Owners to start creating labels and tracking your belongings (if collaborating with the MoveID feature, it may benefit organization to allow the move members who are also Owners to create their own Owner ID to allow for personalized and customized experiences)

•	Generate & Test Your Move ID: Locate your MoveID (located on the Dashboard above the Participants list and in the settings) to share with your family, friends or anyone collaborating on the move. Ensure each member is correctly placed in the intended move.

•	Invite & Onboard Helpers: Send invitations to friends and family who will be assisting. Provide them your MoveID and get your army ready to go.

•	Verify Scanning Functionality: Once all Owners and Spaces have been created, print out the initial batches for each Owner and Space and test everyone’s devices to ensure their cameras are working and the scanning feature is properly working.

•	Establish Your Moving Budget: Whether you’ve already created a budget and just need to digitize it (“Those that Do”) or you have never created a budget before now (“Those that Don’t”), the Budget feature is designed for ease of use. 

•	Get The Timing in Check: Get your moving timeline setup, customize it to your liking by adding, removing and modifying the tasks within the checklist.

•	Save the Date: Utilize the Calendar feature by adding in important dates, events, times, and whatever else you want to track within the app to ensure a smooth move.

8 Weeks Out: Planning and Purging

•	Declutter Every Room: Sort all items into four categories: keep, donate, sell, or trash. Reducing volume will lower costs, save time, and minimize the risk of damage.

•	Determine Required Truck Size: Use your home inventory to select the appropriate truck size. Consider logistics like mileage limits, ramp height, and pickup/return hours.

•	Research Rental Truck Companies: Compare rates, vehicle availability, insurance policies, and pickup/return locations. Pay close attention to any potential holiday surcharges.

•	Gather Quotes for Truck Rentals: Request detailed quotes for your exact move dates. Ensure the quote includes the base rate, mileage fees, fuel policy, and any add-on costs.

•	Outline Your Packing Strategy: Create a timeline for packing, starting with the least-used rooms first. Set weekly goals for the number of boxes to pack to avoid a last-minute rush.

•	Schedule High-Demand Appointments: Book elevator access, donation pickups, and hazardous waste disposal appointments as early as possible to secure your desired time slots.

•	Identify & Plan for Non-Allowable Items: Review your rental truck agreement and local regulations for restricted items (e.g., propane tanks, fuel, chemicals). Make a plan for their proper disposal or alternative transport.

•	Create a Preliminary Floor Plan: Measure large furniture and key doorways at your new home to confirm everything will fit. Planning placement now will save time and effort on move day.

7 Weeks Out: Supplies and Equipment

•	Purchase Packing Materials: Buy boxes, tape, bubble wrap, packing paper, and mattress bags. Get about 15% more than you estimate needing to avoid running out mid-project.

•	Source Free Boxes (Optional): Check with local stores, community groups, or friends for sturdy, clean boxes to reduce supply costs. Avoid any boxes that are damaged or damp.

•	Rent or Buy Moving Equipment: Secure dollies, hand trucks, moving blankets, and straps. These tools are essential for preventing injury and protecting your belongings.

•	Prep Your Packing Stations: Reinforce the bottom of each box with extra tape. Pre-assemble a variety of box sizes and stage them in each room to make packing faster.

•	Begin Selling & Donating Items: List items for sale online and schedule pickups for donations. Keep detailed records of any tax-deductible contributions.

•	Back Up Digital Files: Save important documents, photos, and work files to a cloud service or an external hard drive before your computers and devices are packed.

•	Organize Vital Documents: Create a dedicated "Move File" for physical copies of IDs, medical records, insurance policies, vehicle titles, and contracts.

6 Weeks Out: Logistics and Records

•	Reserve Your Moving Truck: Formally book your vehicle and confirm the pickup/return times. Review the cancellation policy and any potential late fees.

•	Apply for Parking Permits: If required by your city or building, secure parking permits for loading and unloading at both your old and new addresses to avoid fines.

•	Reserve Elevators & Loading Docks: Book exclusive time windows for building access. Confirm all rules, including any requirements for padded elevators.

•	Obtain Medical & Prescription Records: Request records from your current doctors and arrange to have prescriptions transferred to a pharmacy in your new area.

•	Transfer School Records: Coordinate with your children's current and future schools to transfer records. Confirm enrollment deadlines and required documentation.

•	Notify Landlord & Review Lease: Provide written notice to your landlord according to your lease terms. Document all move-out requirements to ensure you get your security deposit back.

•	Finalize Pet Logistics: Schedule a final vet visit, update microchips and tags, and create a clear plan for keeping your pets safe and calm on moving day.

5 Weeks Out: Packing Ramps Up

•	Begin Packing Non-Essential Areas: Start with storage spaces, seasonal items, and decorative objects. Label each box clearly and scan the QR code to log its contents in the app.

•	Label & Inventory Every Box: As you pack, note the room, contents, fragility level, and loading priority for each box. Use QR labels to create a detailed digital inventory.

•	Disassemble Large Furniture: Take apart bed frames, shelves, and large tables. Place all hardware in labeled bags and tape them securely to the corresponding furniture piece.

•	Prepare Your Specialty Item Strategy: Plan the safe transport of sensitive items like TVs, artwork, and instruments. Order specialty crates or boxes if needed.

•	Use Up Perishable Items: Start consuming food from your pantry, fridge, and freezer, as well as cleaning supplies, to minimize what you have to move.

4 Weeks Out: Admin and Appointments

•	File a Change of Address with USPS: Complete the change of address form online and set your mail forwarding start date.

•	Update Your Address with Key Institutions: Notify banks, credit card companies, and insurance providers of your new address to ensure service continuity.

•	Transfer or Cancel Memberships: Contact gyms, clubs, and subscription services to transfer or cancel your accounts and avoid paying for unused services.

•	Schedule Utility Services: Arrange for utilities (electricity, gas, water, internet) to be disconnected at your old home and activated at your new one.

•	Return All Borrowed Items: Take back library books, rented equipment, and anything you've borrowed from friends to avoid fees or last-minute errands.

•	Arrange Childcare & Pet Care for Move Day: Book a sitter or find a safe, quiet space for children and pets to ensure the moving process is efficient and stress-free for everyone.

•	Schedule Move-Out Repairs & Cleaning: Book any necessary services to meet the conditions of your lease or home sale agreement.

3 Weeks Out: Confirmations and Safety

•	Confirm All Truck Reservation Details: Call the rental company to reconfirm your dates, times, and any rented equipment like dollies or blankets. Double-check your insurance coverage.

•	Reconfirm Permits & Building Access: Verify your elevator and loading dock reservations. Ensure you have any required permits or certificates of insurance ready.

•	Measure Critical Pathways: Measure tight doorways, stairwells, and corners at both locations. Identify any furniture that may require special maneuvering.

•	Dispose of Hazardous Materials: Safely dispose of paint, solvents, propane tanks, and other non-allowable items at a designated local facility.

•	Finalize Your Furniture Placement Plan: Lock in your floor plan and share a digital or printed copy with your helpers for clear move-day guidance.

•	Set Up a Move-Day Communication Plan: Create and share a contact list and establish a group chat for real-time updates and coordination.

2 Weeks Out: Final Prep and Staging

•	Pack All Non-Essential Items: Leave out only what you'll need for the next two weeks. Stage packed boxes in their respective rooms, keeping pathways clear.

•	Prepare Equipment & Pathways: Position dollies, blankets, and tools near the exit. Clear hallways and secure rugs to prevent trips and falls.

•	Pack an "Essentials" Box: Fill one or two boxes with first-night necessities like bedding, toiletries, medications, chargers, and basic tools. Keep this box with you in your personal vehicle.

•	Secure Valuables & Important Documents: Gather all jewelry, passports, cash, and sensitive papers to be transported in your personal vehicle.

•	Plan Your Travel & Truck Route: Map out the driving route, noting any low-clearance bridges, fuel stations, and rest stops for the moving truck.

•	Arrange Temporary Housing (If Needed): If there's a gap between your move-out and move-in dates, book a short-term rental.

1 Week Out: The Home Stretch

•	Pack Remaining Belongings: Finish all general packing, leaving out only a go-bag and daily essentials for the final week.

•	Remove Wall Hangings & Patch Holes: Take down all artwork, shelves, and TV mounts. Patch and touch up the paint to meet move-out standards.

•	Deep Clean Your Home: Thoroughly clean your current home after it's empty, or confirm your scheduled cleaning service appointment.

•	Protect Floors & High-Traffic Areas: Lay down protective runners or cardboard in hallways and doorways to prevent scratches and dings.

•	Finalize Plans with Helpers: Reconfirm arrival times and roles with your moving team. Share the final timeline and floor plan one last time.

•	Stage Your Tool Kit: Gather all necessary tools, including a drill, hex keys, extra tape, straps, and a first-aid kit, and keep them easily accessible.

Final Week: Daily Checklist

•	6 Days Out: Verify all building access and permit confirmations.

•	5 Days Out: Consume or donate all remaining perishable food.

•	4 Days Out: Unplug, clean, and prepare all small appliances for packing.

•	3 Days Out: Confirm utility start and stop dates; have a mobile hotspot ready as a backup.

•	2 Days Out: Load your personal vehicle with valuables, documents, and the essentials box.

•	1 Day Out: Defrost and wipe down the fridge and freezer; pack the last remaining items.

Move Day: Hour-by-Hour Guide

•	Early Morning: Pick up the truck and perform a detailed inspection. Take photos of any pre-existing damage and verify the fuel level before you leave the lot.

•	Loading: Begin by loading the heaviest items and appliances first, followed by large furniture, and then boxes. Distribute weight evenly and secure everything with straps.

•	Mid-Load: Use moving blankets and corner guards to protect furniture and doorways. Continue to use safe lifting techniques.

•	Final Walkthrough: Before leaving, check every closet, cabinet, and storage space one last time. Take photos of the property's condition for your records.

•	Arrival: Park the truck legally and brief your helpers on the floor plan. Confirm you have access to the elevator or loading dock.

•	Unloading: Follow the box labels and floor plan to place items in the correct rooms. Keep pathways clear to work efficiently.

•	Post-Unload: Assemble beds and unpack your essentials box. Verify that all utilities are working. Refuel and return the truck on time to avoid extra charges.

Post-Move: First 72 Hours

•	Perform a Safety Check: Test all utilities, smoke detectors, and carbon monoxide detectors. Locate the breaker panel and main water shutoff valve.

•	Unpack High-Priority Rooms: Start by unpacking your essentials, then set up the bedrooms, bathrooms, and kitchen.

•	Break Down Boxes & Return Equipment: Flatten all boxes for recycling and return any rented equipment on time.

Post-Move: First 2 Weeks

•	Update Address & Registrations: Update your driver's license, voter registration, and vehicle registration. Notify your employer to update payroll.

•	Establish Healthcare & Schooling: Register with new doctors, dentists, and veterinarians. Transfer prescriptions and complete school enrollment.

•	Secure Your New Home: Change the locks, install any necessary child or pet safety gates, and note any urgent repairs.

•	Reconcile Your Budget: Log all final moving expenses and compare them against your initial budget to track your spending.

•	Explore Your New Community: Locate essential services, parks, and public transit options. Introduce yourself to your new neighbors.
