# Moving Checklist

## Getting Started

### 1. Set up your Smooth Moves account

### 2. Configure app features and team settings
* Set up owners/spaces with color coding.
* Configure your preferences for notifications and scanning.
* Generate your unique **Move ID** for team collaboration.
* Test QR code scanning functionality on your phone.

Use the app's **Financial Navigator** to establish your DIY moving budget. Choose from pre-built moving templates or create custom categories to track truck rental, packing supplies, fuel, food, and other moving expenses with visual analytics.

## Before Moving: Administrative Tasks

### 1. Change your postal address
Consider changing your postal address prior to Moving day – do it in person at the nearest local post office or complete the important task online. You can also do a change of address over the phone or by mail. Follow a detailed *change of address checklist* to save plenty of time.

### 2. Have your mail forwarded
Ask a good neighbor or a close friend who lives nearby to forward your mail to your new-home address until the change of address procedure takes effect. Leave them some money for the shipping costs.

### 3. Share your updated address
Notify friends, colleagues, and important institutions and organizations of your updated postal address. You can do this in person, over the phone, via e-mail, or through social networks. Here’s a *10-point checklist of who to notify when you move house*.

### 4. Cancel any subscriptions
Remember to cancel any home-delivery services as you’re moving to a new address soon enough. Also, it’s a good idea to discontinue your memberships in gyms, clubs, hobby groups, organizations, and so on.

### 5. Return borrowed items
Return books, films, or any other borrowed items from friends, libraries, or organizations.

### 6. Transfer your utilities
Get in touch with your utility companies and set a date for having the utilities at your current home – electricity, water, gas, phone and Internet services, and central heating – disconnected, and then reconnected at the new home. These *10 steps will show you how to transfer utilities when moving*.

### 7. Speak with your landlord
When moving out of a rented property, your tenant moving out checklist should remind you to speak with your landlord about it and inform them in advance of your plans so that you can collect your security deposit without any additional drama.

### 8. Obtain medical records
Pay a visit to your family physician and dentist, and obtain copies of all your medical records and documentation that will help you register with a new healthcare provider (find a new doctor) in the new town or city you’re moving to.

### 9. Obtain and transfer school records
If you have a school-age child, request his or her records from their school so that you can enroll your kid at a new educational institution upon arrival. To save time, try to arrange a direct inter-school transfer of those documents.

## Before Moving: Personal & Family

### 1. Have a talk with your child or children
Speak with your child or children about the upcoming home move and discuss the changes that are happening soon. Ask them about their hopes and expectations, and do your best to calm down any home-moving fears they may have.

### 2. Face the fears of moving
Moving house can be an intimidating transitional period where you may have to face and overcome a number of moving fears: *fear of the unknown, fear of moving to a new house, fear of moving to a new city, fear of losing what you have right now*, and so on.

### 3. Handle the moving stress
Learn what you can do to handle the stress of moving before and during the big move itself. It’s not a secret that moving house is one of life’s most stressful events, so use these *20 tried-and-true ways to handle stress when moving*.

### 4. Take your pet to the vet
While figuring out the best way to move your dear pet, take them to the vet’s office for a complete medical checkup. Request any immunization records your canine or feline friend may have.

### 5. Pay up any bills and debts
Don’t forget (accidentally, of course) to pay up any remaining home bills or to settle any financial obligations (debts) prior to the move. Ideally, you won’t owe anything to anyone – moving away is a great opportunity to get a fresh start in a new place.

### 6. Reserve the building elevator
When moving out of a high-rise apartment, contact the building manager to reserve the elevator for your moving day – when you and your team will be loading the moving truck.

### 7. Get any repair works done
It’s time to think about any home repairs you have to do or get done before moving out. This way, you’ll get your security deposit back if you’re a renter, or you’ll sell your home for a better price if you’re an owner.

### 8. Create a floor plan
Measure up your biggest furniture pieces to know if they will fit into your new home and match its interior. The floor plan will help you decide what items to move and what to leave behind.

### 9. Say your farewells
Find some time for your friends in your busy residential moving checklist too. Invite your closest pals to a wild pre-move party or plan fun activities with them as a way to say your bittersweet goodbyes.

## Before Moving: DIY Moving Setup & Team Coordination

### 1. Set up your Smooth Moves team
Invite family members, friends, or other helpers to join your move in the app using your unique **Move ID**. This enables real-time collaboration, task assignments, and coordinated planning throughout your DIY move.

### 2. Plan your DIY moving timeline
Use the app's **calendar system** to schedule key moving milestones: packing days, truck rental pickup, moving day, and return deadlines. Set reminders and assign tasks to team members for better coordination.

### 3. Estimate your DIY moving costs
Use **Financial Navigator's** moving budget templates to estimate costs for truck rental, fuel, packing supplies, equipment rental, food, and incidentals. Track actual expenses as you go to stay within budget.

### 4. Consider backup plans
Plan for contingencies in your DIY move:
* What if helpers cancel?
* What if the truck breaks down?
* What if weather delays your move?
Have backup contacts and alternative dates ready.

### 5. Create your box inventory system
Start creating boxes in the app, assigning them to owners/spaces using the color-coded system. Generate QR code labels in batches for efficient organization and tracking throughout your move.

## Before Moving: Do-It-Yourself Move

### 1. Assess your DIY options
When moving only a short distance away, sufficient preparation time, reliable friends, and lack of any special household items may encourage you to try to move home by yourself. *Are you up to the self-move challenge?*

### 2. Do what’s best for you
Assess your DIY moving capacity based on distance, amount of belongings, available help, and physical capabilities. Ensure you have enough people and the right equipment for a successful DIY move.

### 3. Recruit and invite helpers to your move
Reach out to friends and family early to avoid schedule conflicts. Once confirmed, invite them to join your move in the app using your **Move ID** so they can access the digital box system, see the calendar, and coordinate in real-time.

### 4. Secure a moving van
You will need a suitable moving vehicle to transport your belongings – borrow a pickup truck or a van from a friend, or get in touch with a trustworthy truck rental company in order to rent a moving truck of the right size.

### 5. Pick the best truck rental company
Visit the websites of the major truck rental companies in the country – **U-Haul, Penske, Budget, Ryder, and Enterprise** – and check their rates and conditions in order to pick the right truck rental agency for you.

### 6. Get a parking permit
You want the borrowed or hired moving vehicle to be able to park right in front of the entrance of your home. Get a parking permit to avoid headaches because parking in big cities can be a hassle.

### 7. Rent or purchase moving equipment
Our local moving checklist continues with another must-do self-moving task: get your hands on the right type of moving equipment:
* a furniture dolly
* a hand truck
* furniture sliders
* moving blankets
* a set of hand tools

### 8. Secure packing materials
If you intend to pack up your home without hiring professional packers, you will surely need packing supplies of the right quantity and quality. See the packing checklist below so that you can really organize your time.

### 9. Stay safe
Safety is a big concern when organizing a self-move. Follow the proper lifting techniques, use appropriate moving equipment, always have friends to help you lift and carry heavy household items, and keep exit pathways clear.

### 10. Find a fitting solution
Some larger furniture pieces may refuse to go through a door opening. Oops! Try disassembling such a furniture piece to its main components, attempting different exit angles, taking the door off its hinges, or asking experts for assistance if all other options fail.

### 11. Disassemble large and heavy furniture
Check your printable moving checklist to make sure you’re ready to move your furniture to the new home. If needed, take apart safely the larger and heavier furniture pieces you own and pack them separately to save time and avoid damage.

### 12. Load the moving truck
Load the moving vehicle the right way – labeled moving boxes should be loaded first, followed by large furniture pieces, while any heavyweight household appliances should go last. Have your friends’ support and manpower at all times to prevent personal injuries.

### 13. Secure loaded items
Secure well the household items you have just loaded to keep them safe on the road. Strap or rope the larger items to the side of the truck to stop them from moving and possibly getting damaged during transit.

### 14. Get qualified help
Don’t hesitate to call for professional assistance should your decision to move on your own proves to be the wrong one midway through the relocation process.

### 15. Have your car serviced
Have your car serviced at an authorized service station before driving off with your own vehicle in the direction of the new house or apartment

### 16. Plan your car trip
Spend some time planning your own car trip and make travel arrangements. There are many things to think about: the places where you will stop along the way, where you will spend the night(s) if the drive is scheduled to take multiple days, and so on.

### 17. Take your pets with you
Take advantage of your self-move to transport your pets in your own car. Keep in mind that household moving companies will not agree to transport any pet animals for you because the law forbids them to do so.

## Before Moving: Packing Checklist

### 1. Assess your packing options
Using the app's **calendar system**, schedule dedicated packing days and assign specific rooms or box categories to team members. Budget for packing supplies using **Financial Navigator** and organize your DIY packing approach.

### 2. Use a good packing checklist
Packing is time-consuming, so use the app's **box management system** to stay organized. Create boxes digitally before packing, assign them to owners/spaces, and generate QR labels in batches to streamline the process.

### 3. Find free cardboard boxes
Use **Financial Navigator** to budget for packing supplies, then seek free boxes from friends and local businesses like supermarkets and bookstores. Log any savings from free supplies to track your budget performance.

### 4. Get hold of packing materials
Gather quality packing supplies:
* boxes
* soft packing paper
* bubble wrap
* moving blankets
* packing tape
* QR code labels
Record all supply purchases in **Financial Navigator** to track your packing budget.

### 5. Inventory your home
Go room by room and create boxes in the app for items you're moving. Add detailed descriptions, assign owners/spaces using the color-coded system, and mark items requiring special handling. Generate QR code labels for each box.

### 6. Sort out your items
Use your digital box system to categorize items by type, purpose, and condition. Assign items to specific owners or spaces in the app. Set aside things you won't take – *anything unused for 12+ months*.

### 7. Purge your home
Get rid of any items you won't ever need again to save money and time – you can't and shouldn't start packing absolutely everything you own. Sell items online or at a yard sale, then add the income to your **Financial Navigator** budget.

### 8. Organize a moving sale
Don't pay for transporting any items that you won't ever need again. Instead, host a garage sale before moving out to try and sell those unwanted things. Record all earnings in **Financial Navigator** to offset your moving expenses.

### 9. Be charitable
Do consider giving away to friends or donating to charity still usable household items that you won’t take with you. Reduce your transportation costs and help less fortunate people at the same time.

### 10. Do not skimp on packing supplies
Use as many packing supplies as it is necessary to protect your valued items. Be generous with padding materials (packing paper, bubble wrap, etc.), especially when you’re packing fragile items.

### 11. Create an essentials box in the app
Create a special **"Essentials"** box in the app for vital items you'll need first day in your new home. Include toiletries, utensils, medicines, and clothes. Mark this box as high priority and pack it last so it's first to unpack.

### 12. Guard your valuables
Pack high-value articles safely – jewelry, collector's items, important documents, electronics – and transport them personally in your car rather than in the moving truck for maximum security.

### 13. Purchase extra insurance
Consider additional insurance for high-value items you'll transport yourself or with your truck rental. Document valuable items in separate boxes in the app with photos and detailed descriptions for insurance purposes.

### 14. Reinforce your cardboard boxes
Use high-quality packing tape to double-tape the bottoms of all cardboard boxes for extra packing safety, especially if you’re using second-hand containers. *It’s better to be safe than sorry*.

### 15. Do not pack non-allowable items
Do not pack hazardous, perishable, or irreplaceable items in your moving truck. Research truck rental company restrictions and transport important documents, jewelry, and valuables personally in your car.

### 16. Initiate the packing process
Start packing rooms you use least often – storage areas, garages, basements, attics, guest rooms, and closets. Create boxes for these areas first in the app, then pack and apply QR labels to track progress.

### 17. Keep your packing focus
Start early and pack one room at a time using your app-organized system. Use the calendar to schedule packing sessions and track progress. Update box status to **'Packed'** as you complete each one to stay motivated.

### 18. Label all packed boxes
Apply pre-printed QR code labels to each packed box – *no need for hand-written labels!* The app's color-coded system already identifies owners/spaces. Update the box status to **'Packed'** by scanning the QR code.

### 19. Get packing assistance
Coordinate with your team members through the app to get packing help. Assign specific boxes or rooms to different helpers using the calendar system. Complete all packing before Moving day – *no exceptions*.

## Moving Day Checklist
Our ultimate moving checklist continues with the essential tasks you must complete on the day of your move. Follow this detailed moving day checklist in order to guarantee a happy ending to your residential move.

### 1. Defrost your fridge
Let your refrigerator and/or freezer defrost at least 24 hours prior to Moving day. With enough time, you may clean your fridge right there and then, although it’ll make more sense to do it after the move.

### 2. Keep your phone close by
Keep your phone fully charged and ready for QR code scanning. Ensure the **Smooth Moves app** is updated and working properly. You'll be scanning boxes throughout the day to update their status.

### 3. Get a good night’s sleep
Go to bed early and get good rest before your DIY moving day. You'll need energy, stamina, and concentration to coordinate your team, lift boxes, and manage the entire move yourself.

### 4. Start Moving day early
Don’t make any costly Moving day mistakes like sleeping in. Instead, wake up early on Moving day to have some extra time to get everything ready. Take a deep breath and do everything in your power to keep your cool during one of life’s most stressful and unpredictable days.

### 5. Wear appropriate clothes
Wear comfortable clothes that won’t restrict your movements but will still provide decent protection. Have closed footwear with anti-slip soles, preferably sports shoes. Avoid baggy clothes, jewelry, and any loose accessories.

### 6. Ensure Moving day safety
Moving days can be plain dangerous. Make sure small kids and pets are cordoned off in a safe place in the home, far from the house-moving action.

### 7. Finish last-minute tasks
Complete any final preparations before your helpers arrive. Check the app for any unfinished box preparations and ensure all team members know their assigned roles and arrival times.

### 8. Coordinate your DIY team
Welcome your helpers and volunteers as they arrive. Give everyone access to the app, show them how to scan QR codes to update box status, and assign specific areas or tasks. Provide refreshments and establish clear communication.

### 9. Clean the home you’re leaving
Follow a good home cleaning checklist to clean up the place before moving out in order to collect your security deposit (renters) or have a smooth home sale process (owners). Dispose of the cleaning products properly after that highly unattractive task.

### 10. Do the final security check
Take a final walk around your home to make sure everything looks okay, and that nothing (or no one!) is left behind. Check whether all windows and doors are closed and locked up, and that electricity, water, and gas supplies are switched off before you move out.

### 11. Start the relocation journey
Say goodbye to your old home and have a safe relocation journey to your new house or apartment, and your new life chapter as well.

## After Moving: New Home Checklist
You’ve reached your new home safely, so congratulations are in order. Moving day may be over, but the staggering number of post-relocation jobs (21? Did somebody say 21?) are yet to come.

### 1. Check household utilities
Check if all home utilities are up and running – electricity, water, gas, cable TV, phone, and so on. Write down the readings of all utility meters. Contact your new utility providers if there are any problems.

### 2. Unpack any essentials boxes
Unpack your open-first boxes and use the essential items inside them to survive the first night in the new home or until you get reunited with your everyday household items. Switch to survival mode, if you must.

### 3. Check delivered boxes
Scan QR codes and update box status to **'Delivered'** as you unload items. Use the app to track which boxes have arrived and check for any damage or missing items using your digital inventory.

### 4. Finalize truck rental and expenses
Complete your truck rental return and settle any final expenses (fuel, equipment rental, etc.). Record all final costs in **Financial Navigator** to complete your budget tracking and analyze total move costs.

### 5. Thank your helpers
Show appreciation to friends and family who helped with your DIY move. Consider treating them to dinner or giving small thank-you gifts for their time and effort in making your move successful.

### 6. Tend to your pets
Don’t neglect the needs of your pets – give them enough time to acclimatize properly by placing them in a safe room and showing them how much you care for them.

### 7. Childproof the new home
If you just moved house with a small baby or a toddler, it’s vital that you baby-proof the new home so that it becomes 100% safe for your little angel to play and grow inside it.

### 8. Create a post-move checklist
Use the app's **calendar** to track post-move tasks and deadlines. Schedule important activities like unpacking priority rooms, setting up utilities, and registering with new services. The end of Moving day is just the beginning of settling in.

### 9. Start unpacking
Begin unpacking using your QR code system. Scan boxes as you unpack them and update status to **'Unpacked'**. Prioritize essentials boxes and rooms you need immediately (bedroom, bathroom, kitchen). Coordinate with team members through the app.

### 10. Look for a new job
It’s time to update your CV and go job-hunting if you haven’t secured a new job in the new city or town. Many post-move tasks are important but nothing comes close to securing a constant income.

### 11. Find a good school
Register your school-age child or children at a reputable school if you haven’t done so prior to the move. Do school tours in person to find the most suitable school for your son or daughter.

### 12. Register with healthcare providers
Your health is your top priority – do not delay the task of finding a family physician, a dentist, and a veterinarian (if applicable). Take all the required documentation with you for the registration process.

### 13. Register your car
Register your vehicle if you just moved to a new state. You may also need to renew your driver’s license, so it’s better to contact the local DMV office for more information.

### 14. Be thrifty after the move
Don’t overspend unnecessarily right after the move by rushing to various stores to buy whatever you think you need for the new place. Instead, your post-move moving checklist should encourage you to wait some time to actually see whether or not you will really need all those new-house items.

### 15. Throw a housewarming party
Consider organizing a housewarming party to get to know your new work colleagues and neighbors and hopefully find a few friends in them. Thus said, don’t forget to keep in touch with your old friends too.

### 16. Get to know your new city
Be open and receptive to the new environment in order to avoid the dangerous clutches of relocation depression. Explore your new city little by little and locate important landmarks to help you adjust faster.

### 17. Make new friends
Be open to new friendships after moving to a new city or town but don’t forget to keep in touch with your old pals either. Moving house is a tough ordeal, so you’ll need all the friends you can get to make it.

### 18. Review your DIY moving experience
Consider sharing your DIY moving experience and tips with others, especially about using digital tools like **Smooth Moves**. Your insights could help friends and family plan their own successful DIY moves.
