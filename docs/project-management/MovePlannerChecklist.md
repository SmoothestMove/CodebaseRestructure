## App Setup & Initial Planning

### **Get Comfortable with the App**
Take time to explore the app's layout, features, and navigation.

### **Configure App Settings**
Set up owners/spaces, utilizing color coding for better organization. Configure your personal preferences for notifications and scanning.

### **Assess Your DIY Move Capacity**
Before committing, assess your move based on distance, amount of belongings, available help, and your physical capabilities to ensure a self-move is right for you.

### **Define Spaces & Create Owners**
Organize your move by adding rooms to the Spaces page as needed and create Owners to start creating labels and tracking your belongings.

### **Generate & Test Your Move ID**
Locate your MoveID and share it with anyone collaborating on the move. Ensure each member is correctly placed in the intended move.

### **Invite & Onboard Helpers**
Reach out to friends and family early to avoid schedule conflicts. Send invitations and your MoveID to get your team ready to go.

### **Verify Scanning Functionality**
Print out initial batches of QR labels for each Owner and Space and test everyone’s devices to ensure their cameras and the scanning feature are working properly.

### **Establish Your Moving Budget**
Use the app's Financial Navigator/Budget feature. Whether you digitize an existing budget or create a new one, choose from pre-built moving templates or create custom categories to track expenses.

### **Set Up Your Timeline & Calendar**
Get your moving timeline set up in the checklist, customizing it as needed. Use the Calendar feature to add important dates, events, and deadlines.

### **Consider Backup Plans**
Plan for contingencies: What if helpers cancel? What if the truck breaks down? What if weather delays your move? Have backup contacts and alternative dates ready.

## 8 Weeks Out: Planning, Purging & Well-being

### **Address the Emotional Side of Moving**
Have a talk with your child or children about the move to discuss the changes and calm any fears. Take time to acknowledge and face the common fears of moving and learn ways to handle the stress.

### **Declutter Every Room**
Go room by room and sort all items into four categories: keep, donate, sell, or trash. A good rule is to get rid of anything unused for 12+ months.

### **Pay Up Bills & Debts**
Review your finances and settle any remaining home bills or financial obligations for a clean financial start.

### **Determine Required Truck Size**
Use your home inventory to select the appropriate truck size, considering logistics like mileage limits and ramp height.

### **Research Rental Truck Companies**
Compare rates, availability, and policies for companies like U-Haul, Penske, Budget, Ryder, and Enterprise.

### **Gather Quotes for Truck Rentals**
Request detailed quotes for your exact move dates, ensuring they include the base rate, mileage fees, and any add-on costs.

### **Outline Your Packing Strategy**
Create a timeline for packing, starting with the least-used rooms first. Set weekly goals for the number of boxes to pack to avoid a last-minute rush.

### **Schedule High-Demand Appointments**
Book elevator access, donation pickups, and hazardous waste disposal appointments as early as possible.

### **Identify & Plan for Non-Allowable Items**
Review your rental agreement and local regulations for restricted items (e.g., propane tanks, fuel, chemicals) and plan for their proper disposal.

### **Create a Preliminary Floor Plan**
Measure large furniture and key doorways at your new home to confirm everything will fit. This will help you decide what to move and what to leave behind.

## 7 Weeks Out: Supplies and Equipment

### **Purchase Packing Materials**
Buy boxes, tape, bubble wrap, packing paper, and mattress bags. Be generous with padding materials, especially when packing fragile items.

### **Source Free Boxes (Optional)**
Check with local stores, community groups, or friends for sturdy, clean boxes to reduce supply costs.

### **Rent or Buy Moving Equipment**
Secure dollies, a hand truck, moving blankets, furniture sliders, and straps.

### **Prep Your Packing Stations**
Reinforce the bottom of each box with extra tape. Pre-assemble a variety of box sizes and stage them in each room to make packing faster.

### **Begin Selling & Donating Items**
List items for sale online, organize a moving sale, and schedule pickups for donations. Add any income to your budget in the app.

### **Be Charitable**
Consider giving away still-usable household items that you won’t take with you to friends or donating them to charity.

## 6 Weeks Out: Logistics and Records

### **Reserve Your Moving Truck**
Formally book your vehicle and confirm pickup/return times. Review the cancellation policy and any potential late fees.

### **Back Up Digital Files**
Save important documents, photos, and work files to a cloud service or an external hard drive.

### **Organize Vital Documents**
Create a dedicated "Move File" for physical copies of IDs, medical records, insurance policies, vehicle titles, and contracts.

### **Apply for Parking Permits**
Secure any required parking permits for both addresses to avoid fines or hassles.

### **Reserve Elevators & Loading Docks**
Book exclusive time windows for building access and confirm all rules, including any requirements for padded elevators.

### **Obtain Medical & Prescription Records**
Request records from your current doctors and arrange to have prescriptions transferred to a new pharmacy.

### **Transfer School Records**
Coordinate with schools to transfer records and confirm enrollment deadlines.

### **Notify Landlord & Review Lease**
Provide written notice to your landlord according to your lease terms and document all move-out requirements.

### **Finalize Pet Logistics**
Schedule a final vet visit, update microchips and tags, and create a clear plan for keeping your pets safe and calm on moving day.

## 5 Weeks Out: Packing Ramps Up

### **Begin Packing Non-Essential Areas**
Start with storage spaces, garages, basements, seasonal items, and decorative objects.

### **Label & Inventory Every Box**
Use pre-printed QR code labels. As you pack, note the room, contents, fragility level, and loading priority for each box in the app to create a detailed digital inventory.

### **Disassemble Large Furniture**
Take apart bed frames, shelves, and large tables. Place all hardware in labeled bags and tape them securely to the corresponding furniture piece.

### **Prepare Your Specialty Item Strategy**
Plan the safe transport of sensitive items like TVs, artwork, and instruments. Consider purchasing extra insurance for these high-value items.

### **Use Up Perishable Items**
Start consuming food from your pantry, fridge, and freezer to minimize what you have to move.

## 4 Weeks Out: Admin and Appointments

### **File a Change of Address with USPS**
Complete the change of address form online and set your mail forwarding start date.

### **Update Your Address with Key Institutions**
Notify banks, credit card companies, insurance providers, and other important institutions of your new address.

### **Transfer or Cancel Memberships**
Contact gyms, clubs, and subscription services to transfer or cancel your accounts.

### **Schedule Utility Services**
Arrange for utilities (electricity, gas, water, internet) to be disconnected at your old home and activated at your new one.

### **Return All Borrowed Items**
Take back library books, rented equipment, and anything borrowed from friends.

### **Arrange Childcare & Pet Care for Move Day**
Book a sitter or find a safe, quiet space for children and pets.

### **Schedule Move-Out Repairs & Cleaning**
Book any necessary services to meet the conditions of your lease or home sale agreement.

### **Plan Your Farewells**
Find time to see your closest friends for a pre-move party or fun activities to say your goodbyes.

## 2-3 Weeks Out: Confirmations & Final Prep

### **Confirm All Reservations**
Call the rental company to reconfirm your truck, dates, times, and any rented equipment. Reconfirm your elevator and loading dock reservations and permits.

### **Measure Critical Pathways**
Measure tight doorways, stairwells, and corners at both locations. Identify any furniture that may require special maneuvering, and plan your solution (e.g., trying different angles, taking a door off its hinges).

### **Finalize Your Furniture Placement Plan**
Lock in your floor plan and share a digital or printed copy with your helpers for clear move-day guidance.

### **Dispose of Hazardous Materials**
Safely dispose of paint, solvents, propane tanks, and other non-allowable items at a designated local facility.

### **Have Your Car Serviced**
If driving your own vehicle, have it serviced at an authorized service station before your trip.

### **Plan Your Travel & Truck Route**
Map out the driving route for the moving truck and your personal car trip, noting fuel stations and rest stops.

### **Pack All Non-Essential Items**
Leave out only what you'll need for the next two weeks. Stage packed boxes in their respective rooms, keeping pathways clear.

### **Pack an "Essentials" Box**
Create a special "Essentials" box in the app. Fill it with first-night necessities like bedding, toiletries, medications, chargers, and basic tools.

### **Secure Valuables & Important Documents**
Gather all jewelry, passports, cash, and sensitive papers to be transported personally in your car for maximum security.

## 1 Week Out: The Home Stretch

### **Finalize Plans with Helpers**
Reconfirm arrival times and roles with your moving team. Share the final timeline and floor plan one last time.

### **Pack Remaining Belongings**
Finish all general packing, leaving out only a go-bag and daily essentials.

### **Remove Wall Hangings & Patch Holes**
Take down all artwork, shelves, and TV mounts. Patch and touch up the paint to meet move-out standards.

### **Deep Clean Your Home**
Thoroughly clean your current home after it's empty, or confirm your scheduled cleaning service.

### **1 Day Out**
Defrost and wipe down the fridge and freezer. Pack the last remaining items. Load your personal vehicle with valuables, documents, and the essentials box.

## Move Day: Hour-by-Hour Guide

### **Get a Good Night’s Sleep & Start Early**
Go to bed early the night before and wake up early on moving day to have extra time.

### **Dress Appropriately**
Wear comfortable clothes that won’t restrict your movements and closed-toe shoes with anti-slip soles.

### **Pick Up the Truck**
Perform a detailed inspection of the truck. Take photos of any pre-existing damage and verify the fuel level before leaving the lot.

### **Ensure Safety**
Keep small kids and pets cordoned off in a safe, quiet place, far from the moving action. Keep pathways clear to prevent trips and falls.

### **Load the Truck Correctly**
Begin by loading labeled moving boxes first, followed by large furniture pieces, and then the heaviest household appliances last. Distribute weight evenly. Secure everything with straps to stop items from moving during transit.

### **Final Walkthrough & Security Check**
Before leaving, check every closet, cabinet, and storage space one last time. Make sure all windows and doors are closed and locked, and that electricity, water, and gas supplies are switched off. Take photos of the property's condition for your records.

### **Arrival & Unloading**
Brief your helpers on the floor plan. Follow box labels to place items in the correct rooms.

### **Post-Unload**
Assemble beds and unpack your essentials box. Verify that all utilities are working. Refuel and return the truck on time. Thank your helpers for their time and effort.

## Post-Move: Settling In

### **First 72 Hours**
* **Check Utilities & Perform a Safety Check:** Test all utilities, smoke detectors, and carbon monoxide detectors. Locate the breaker panel and main water shutoff valve.
* **Check Delivered Boxes:** Scan QR codes as you unload to update their status. Use the app's digital inventory to check for any damage or missing items.
* **Unpack High-Priority Rooms:** Start with your essentials, then set up the bedrooms, bathrooms, and kitchen.
* **Tend to Your Pets:** Give pets enough time to acclimatize by placing them in a safe room with their belongings.
* **Childproof the New Home:** If you have a small baby or a toddler, it’s vital that you baby-proof the new home so it is 100% safe.
* **Break Down Boxes & Return Equipment:** Flatten all boxes for recycling and return any rented equipment on time.

### **First 2 Weeks**
* **Update Registrations & Address:** Update your driver's license, voter registration, and vehicle registration. Notify your employer to update payroll.
* **Establish Healthcare & Schooling:** Register with new doctors, dentists, and veterinarians. Complete school enrollment.
* **Secure Your New Home:** Change the locks and install any necessary safety gates.
* **Finalize Your Budget:** Log all final moving expenses in the app's Financial Navigator and compare them against your initial budget to track your spending.

### **First Month & Beyond**
* **Explore & Connect:** Explore your new city and locate important landmarks. Be open to new friendships by introducing yourself to neighbors.
* **Throw a Housewarming Party:** Consider organizing a party to get to know new colleagues and neighbors.
* **Review Your DIY Moving Experience:** Consider sharing your DIY moving experience and tips with others.
