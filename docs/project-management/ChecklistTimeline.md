# Moving Checklist Timeline

This timeline organizes your moving tasks into a weekly schedule, starting 8 weeks before your move date.

## 8 Weeks Out: Planning and Purging

*   **Set up your Smooth Moves account:** Create your account and set up your move in the **Smooth Moves app**.
*   **Configure app features and team settings:** Set up owners/spaces, configure notifications, generate your **Move ID**, and test QR code scanning.
*   **Set up your moving budget in Financial Navigator:** Use the app's **Financial Navigator** to establish your DIY moving budget.
*   **Assess your DIY options:** Decide if a DIY move is right for you.
*   **Inventory your home:** Go room-by-room and create boxes in the app.
*   **Sort out your items:** Categorize items by type, purpose, and condition.
*   **Purge your home:** Get rid of any items you won't need again.
*   **Organize a moving sale:** Host a garage sale to sell unwanted items.
*   **Be charitable:** Donate usable items you won't take with you.

## 6 Weeks Out: Logistics and Packing Prep

*   **Recruit and invite helpers to your move:** Reach out to friends and family for help.
*   **Secure a moving van:** Rent a moving truck of the right size.
*   **Pick the best truck rental company:** Research and choose a truck rental company.
*   **Get a parking permit:** Obtain a parking permit for the moving truck.
*   **Rent or purchase moving equipment:** Get dollies, hand trucks, sliders, blankets, and tools.
*   **Secure packing materials:** Gather boxes, packing paper, bubble wrap, and tape.
*   **Find free cardboard boxes:** Look for free boxes to save money.
*   **Do not skimp on packing supplies:** Use enough supplies to protect your items.
*   **Reinforce your cardboard boxes:** Double-tape the bottoms of all boxes.
*   **Do not pack non-allowable items:** Research what you can and cannot pack in the truck.
*   **Initiate the packing process:** Start packing rooms you use least often.
*   **Keep your packing focus:** Pack one room at a time.
*   **Label all packed boxes:** Apply QR code labels to each box.
*   **Get packing assistance:** Coordinate with your team for packing help.
*   **Create a floor plan:** Measure furniture to ensure it will fit in your new home.
*   **Disassemble large and heavy furniture:** Take apart large furniture to make it easier to move.

## 4 Weeks Out: Notifications and Appointments

*   **Change your postal address:** Change your address with the post office.
*   **Have your mail forwarded:** Ask a neighbor or friend to forward your mail.
*   **Share your updated address:** Notify friends, family, and institutions of your new address.
*   **Cancel any subscriptions:** Cancel home-delivery services and memberships.
*   **Return borrowed items:** Return items to friends, libraries, and organizations.
*   **Transfer your utilities:** Schedule disconnection and reconnection of utilities.
*   **Speak with your landlord:** Inform your landlord of your move-out date.
*   **Obtain medical records:** Get copies of your medical records.
*   **Obtain and transfer school records:** Request and transfer your child's school records.
*   **Have a talk with your child or children:** Discuss the move with your kids.
*   **Face the fears of moving:** Acknowledge and address any moving-related anxieties.
*   **Handle the moving stress:** Use stress-management techniques to cope with the pressure of moving.
*   **Take your pet to the vet:** Get a checkup and obtain records for your pet.
*   **Pay up any bills and debts:** Settle any outstanding financial obligations.
*   **Reserve the building elevator:** Reserve the elevator if you're moving out of an apartment building.
*   **Get any repair works done:** Complete any necessary repairs before moving out.
*   **Say your farewells:** Plan farewell gatherings with friends.

## 2 Weeks Out: Final Preparations

*   **Create an essentials box in the app:** Pack a box with items you'll need on the first day.
*   **Guard your valuables:** Pack and transport valuable items personally.
*   **Purchase extra insurance:** Consider additional insurance for valuable items.
*   **Have your car serviced:** Get your car checked before a long-distance move.
*   **Plan your car trip:** Plan your route and any necessary stops.
*   **Take your pets with you:** Plan to transport your pets in your own vehicle.

## 1 Week Out: The Home Stretch

*   **Defrost your fridge:** Defrost your refrigerator and freezer at least 24 hours before moving day.
*   **Get a good night’s sleep:** Rest up before the big day.
*   **Finish last-minute tasks:** Complete any remaining preparations.

## Moving Day: Execution

*   **Keep your phone close by:** Keep your phone charged and ready for QR code scanning.
*   **Start Moving day early:** Wake up early to have extra time.
*   **Wear appropriate clothes:** Wear comfortable and protective clothing.
*   **Ensure Moving day safety:** Keep kids and pets in a safe area.
*   **Coordinate your DIY team:** Welcome your helpers and assign tasks.
*   **Load the moving truck:** Load the truck strategically.
*   **Secure loaded items:** Secure items in the truck to prevent damage.
*   **Clean the home you’re leaving:** Clean your old home before you leave.
*   **Do the final security check:** Make sure you haven't left anything behind.
*   **Start the relocation journey:** Say goodbye to your old home and head to your new one.

## After Moving: Settling In

*   **Check household utilities:** Ensure all utilities are working.
*   **Unpack any essentials boxes:** Unpack your essentials box first.
*   **Check delivered boxes:** Make sure all your boxes have arrived.
*   **Finalize truck rental and expenses:** Return the truck and settle your budget.
*   **Thank your helpers:** Show your appreciation to your moving team.
*   **Tend to your pets:** Help your pets acclimate to the new home.
*   **Childproof the new home:** Make your new home safe for children.
*   **Create a post-move checklist:** Track your post-move tasks.
*   **Start unpacking:** Unpack your boxes.
*   **Look for a new job:** Start your job search if necessary.
*   **Find a good school:** Enroll your children in a new school.
*   **Register with healthcare providers:** Find a new doctor, dentist, and veterinarian.
*   **Register your car:** Register your car and update your driver's license.
*   **Be thrifty after the move:** Avoid unnecessary spending.
*   **Throw a housewarming party:** Celebrate your new home.
*   **Get to know your new city:** Explore your new surroundings.
*   **Make new friends:** Be open to meeting new people.
*   **Review your DIY moving experience:** Share your moving experience with others.

## Adapting the Timeline

If you have less than 8 weeks to prepare, you'll need to condense the timeline. Here’s a guide to prioritizing tasks based on a shorter timeframe:

### 6 Weeks to Go

*   **Weeks 1-2 (Combine 8 & 6-week tasks):** Immediately start with the "8 Weeks Out" tasks (budgeting, decluttering) and the "6 Weeks Out" tasks (booking a truck, gathering supplies). This will be an intensive two weeks, but it's crucial to get these foundational tasks done quickly.
*   **Weeks 3-6:** Follow the timeline as outlined from the "4 Weeks Out" mark.

### 4 Weeks to Go

*   **Week 1 (Combine 8, 6, & 4-week tasks):** This will be your most critical week. You need to accomplish all tasks from the 8, 6, and 4-week sections.
    *   **Priority 1 (Day 1-3):** Focus on the absolute essentials: booking a moving truck, getting packing supplies, and starting the decluttering process.
    *   **Priority 2 (Day 4-7):** Work on administrative tasks like changing your address, notifying institutions, and scheduling utility transfers.
*   **Weeks 2-4:** Follow the timeline from the "2 Weeks Out" mark.

### 2 Weeks to Go

*   **Week 1 (Combine 8, 6, 4, & 2-week tasks):** You'll need to be extremely organized and efficient.
    *   **Priority 1 (Day 1-2):** Secure a moving truck and packing supplies. This is non-negotiable.
    *   **Priority 2 (Day 3-4):** Begin packing non-essential items and decluttering as you go.
    *   **Priority 3 (Day 5-7):** Handle all administrative tasks (address change, utilities, etc.).
*   **Week 2:** Follow the "1 Week Out" and "Moving Day" checklists.

### 1 Week or Less

At this point, you're in "triage mode." Focus on the absolute necessities.

*   **Immediately:**
    *   Book a moving truck or arrange for transportation.
    *   Gather packing supplies.
    *   Recruit any available help from friends and family.
*   **Next 2-3 Days:**
    *   Pack an "essentials" box.
    *   Pack as much as you can, focusing on one room at a time. Don't worry about perfect organization; just get things in boxes.
    *   Handle critical administrative tasks: change of address and utility transfers.
*   **The Day Before:**
    *   Pack remaining items.
    *   Defrost the fridge.
*   **Moving Day:**
    *   Follow the "Moving Day" checklist as closely as possible.

The key to a successful short-notice move is to be ruthless in your decluttering and to accept that some things may not be perfectly organized. Focus on getting your belongings to your new home safely and worry about the rest after you've moved.
